**Preliminary:** What is the single most important element that must undergird our efforts to defend the Christian faith? Is it our eloquence? Our knowledge? According to 1 Peter 3:15, the answer lies in this truth of the Christian faith: "in your hearts sanctify Christ as Lord." This is the foundational principle, ultimate commitment, and supreme motivation behind all true Christian apologetics.

---

## Reformed Classical Apologetics Defined

### Reformed

This approach is, first of all, Reformed. The term "Reformed" means that this apologetic approach is structured within and consistent with the systematic theological tradition of the Reformed Orthodoxy/Scholasticism. This is characterized by a commitment to the Reformed confessions (e.g. 1689 Baptist Confession and the Westminster Confession of Faith) and their theological assumptions.

Scott Swain writes,

> Viewed from the perspective of its historic confessions, Reformed theology may be identified as a catholic, Protestant tradition of inquiry concerning God and all things in relation to God that takes Holy Scripture as its principal source and norm and that orders itself to the glory of God as its chief end. Reformed theology, thus understood, makes specific claims regarding the catholic substance of the faith as summarized in the Ecumenical Creeds, the Ten Commandments, and the Lord's Prayer. Reformed theology exhibits fundamental agreement with Roman Catholicism and other confessional Protestant traditions on what Martin Luther called 'articles of majesty': the being and attributes of the triune God and the person of Jesus Christ. (Allen 2020: 2–3)

Reformed classical apologetics embraces the rigorous, rational, and philosophical methodology of the classical two-step approach while ensuring that this method is expressed and constrained by the doctrines characteristic of Reformed theology (e.g., the authority of Scripture, the effects of sin, and God's sovereignty). Therefore, this approach proceeds from the standpoint of *faith seeking understanding* (Augustinian tradition) rather than pure philosophical autonomy. This is also represented by B.B. Warfield,

> But certainly, before we draw it from the Scriptures, we must assure ourselves that there is a knowledge of God in the Scriptures. And, before, we do that, we must assure ourselves that there is a knowledge of God in the world. And, before we do that, we must assure ourselves that a knowledge of God is possible for man. And, before we do that, we must assure ourselves that there is a God to know. Thus, we inevitably work back to first principles. And, in working thus back to first principles, we exhibit the indispensability of an "Apologetical Theology," which of necessity holds the place of the first among the five essential theological disciplines. (Warfield in Beattie 1904: 24)

### Apologetics

Second, we define Apologetics as the reasoned defense and commendation of the foundational truths of the Christian religion as objectively true, rationally compelling, and existentially applicable to life.

The word "apologetics" comes from the Greek ἀπολογία, meaning "defense" or "vindication". In the New Testament, *apologia* was a formal courtroom defense (Acts 22:1 — before the Sanhedrin; 25:16; 1 Corinthians 9:3; 2 Corinthians 7:11; Philippians 1:7, 16; 2 Timothy 4:16; 1 Peter 3:15).

**Bernard Wuellner, S.J.**

> apologetics, n. an historico-philosophical explanation and defense of the presuppositions and origins of the Christian religion. Apologetics is variously treated as an introduction to theology, as part of the philosophy of religion, and as a selection of topics from the philosophy of man, natural theology, and the history of Christ and the Apostolic Age. (Wuellner, S.J. 1966: 17)

**Sproul, Gerstner, & Lindsley.**

> "Apologetics is the reasoned defense of the Christian religion. Christianity is a faith, to be sure, but there are reasons for this faith. Faith is not to be confused with reason, but neither is it to be separated from it… As a science, it is a putting together of the data common to all Christians in a consistent and scientific whole. It explains why Christians are Christians and why non-Christians should be Christians. If theology is the queen of the sciences, apologetics is her handmaid. It introduces people to the queen and demonstrates her majesty." (Sproul 1984: 13, 16)

**Francis Beattie.**

> "Apologetics presents a reasoned defence and vindication of the essential truths of supernatural origin, divine authority, and inherent sufficiency of the Christian system of doctrine, of worship, of ethics, and of redemption, together with the systematic refutation of all opposing systems." (Beattie 1903: 56)

- Apologetics is the reasoned defense of the entire Christian religion. Christianity is not irrational.
- Apologetics is a philosophical and intellectual discipline concerned with defending the Christian faith against falsehood, inconsistency, or credulity charges.

### Classical

Classical apologetics refers to an overarching methodology characterized by a two-step approach.

**Step One (Philosophical Apologetics via Natural Theology):** This first step seeks to establish the necessary existence and certain core attributes of God (monotheism) through philosophical arguments found in nature and reason, independent of special revelation. These arguments include cosmological, teleological, moral, and ontological proofs, collectively known as *natural theology*. This demonstrates the God of [classical theism](/blog/2026-01-09-what-is-classical-theism).

**Step Two (Evidential Apologetics via Christian Evidences):** Once the foundation of monotheism is established (rendering the possibility of miraculous divine action plausible), the argument moves to demonstrate the truth of specific Christian claims, such as the historical reliability of the Bible, the claims of Jesus, and the reality of the resurrection.

**Douglas Groothuis.**

> "Classical apologetics is the ministry of defending and commending the knowledge of God that is revealed in the Bible and in the world around us. Apologetics shows how Christianity is true, rational, and pertinent to every area of life. The classical strategy begins by first establishing the existence of God as revealed in the world around us, showing how the picture of God as revealed apart from the Bible actually corresponds in some important respects to the God we see in the pages of the Bible. The way apologists do this is called natural theology, which is rational reflection on God's general revelation… Apologetics examines the life and ministry of Jesus of Nazareth, showing that he is God through his claims, credentials, and deeds. This is accomplished by showing the historical reliability of the Bible and by explaining that the Bible's conclusions about Jesus are true." (Groothuis 2023: 6–7)

Classical Apologetics first establishes God's existence, then the life and ministry of Jesus Christ.

## Reformed Classical Apologetics' Biblical Basis: The Lord of Apologetics

> But even if you should suffer for what is right, you are blessed. "Do not fear what they fear; do not be shaken." But in your hearts, sanctify Christ as Lord. Always be prepared to give a defense to everyone who asks you the reason for the hope that is in you. But respond with gentleness and respect, keeping a clear conscience, so that those who slander you may be put to shame by your good behavior in Christ. For it is better, if it is God's will, to suffer for doing good than for doing evil.
>
> — 1 Peter 3:14–17 (Berean Standard Bible)

**Context:** The Christian's response to suffering (vv. 13, 17) faced by being zealous for good works is not fear of men (v. 14) but fear of God in holiness and readiness of heart. This is only possible if Christ is ultimate in our hearts.

**Note:** The Greek text fronts the word Lord "Κύριον" in the accusative form and "emphatically placed forward as predicate" (Alford's *Greek New Testament* 4.362). This shows emphasis on Christ's lordship. Apologetics bows to Christ's lordship. Apologetics seeks to honor Christ in six ways:

1. *Apologetics must be committed to the Lordship of Christ*
   - 1 Peter 3:15 is quoted from Isaiah 8:13, where it directly states that the LORD "YHWH" יְהֹוָה must be honored קָדַשׁ (set apart) and that the Lord of hosts alone be our fear, and "he will become a sanctuary and a stone of offense and a rock of stumbling" (8:14). Peter cited this in 1 Peter 2:8 to refer to Jesus Christ.
   - He is identifying Christ with Yahweh, the God of Israel, who alone deserves absolute commitment, for there is none other besides Him.
   - Our ultimate priority is to never give up our commitment to the glory of God in the face of Jesus Christ.
   - "Apologetics needs to be applied to the whole of life under the lordship of Jesus Christ." (Groothuis 2011: 649)

2. *Apologetics must be a defense of the truth about God and all things in relation to Himself.*
   - We should not leave the world to the atheists, skeptics, and unbelievers.
   - This is our Father's world, and everything in this cosmos reveals who He is.
   - Philosophy serves theology towards doxology. We do not reject philosophy simply because the "pagans" taught it. They are created in God's image, and whatever features of truth they were able to teach came from the hands of God's providence.
   - As stated by Dr. Groothuis above, we demonstrate that "Christianity is true, rational, and pertinent to every area of life."

3. *Apologetics must advance intellectual support for the Christian faith*
   - For evangelicals, the goal of apologetics is not to worship the idea of a god that is the result of some philosophical argument. The goal is to provide intellectual support for my belief in God and to show others that there is a God who exists. (Groothuis 2023)
   - Apologetics necessarily silences "obstreperous mouths" and prevents obstacles to the truth of the Christian religion.

4. *Apologetics must be approached with reverence, gentleness, and humility*
   - We cannot approach apologetics arrogantly. The Christian must have the heart and mind of Christ.
   - **Francis Beattie.** "The subjects with which Apologetics deals are the noblest about which the human mind can be engaged, and the issues involved in Christianity are of immense import. No flippant temper, no irreverent spirit, no half-hearted manner, are in harmony with the discussions in which Apologetics must engage." (Beattie 1903: 45)

5. *Apologetics must be done for the edification of the body of Christ*
   - Apologetics is not done to advance one's own agenda. It must be done to aid, assist, and support other believers in their Christian walk in the midst of this evil world.
   - **J.P. Moreland.** "For one thing, it gives them confidence that their faith is true and reasonable; therefore, apologetics encourages a life of faith seeking understanding. Further, apologetics can actually encourage spiritual growth. A person's ability to grow in Christ is in some measure dependent on what that person is able to see in the Scriptures and the world around him." (Moreland 1987)

6. *Apologetics must depend on the work of the Holy Spirit for the full persuasion of the faith.*
   - But dependence on the Holy Spirit does not mean that we do not toil to provide a clear, compelling, and rational defense of our faith.
   - "The Holy Spirit does not work a blind, an ungrounded faith in the heart. What is supplied by his creative energy in working faith is not a ready-made faith, rooted in nothing, and clinging without reason to its object; nor yet new grounds of belief in the object presented; but just a new ability of the heart to respond to the grounds of faith, sufficient in themselves, already present to the understanding. We believe in Christ because it is rational to believe in him, not though it be irrational." (Warfield in Beattie 1904: 26)
   - "Faith is the gift of God; but it does not in the least follow that the faith that God gives is an irrational faith, that is, a faith without grounds in right reason. It is beyond all question only the prepared heart that can fitly respond to the 'reasons'; but how can even a prepared heart respond, when there are no 'reasons' to draw out its action?" (B.B. Warfield in Beattie 1904: 25)

**Point:** True apologetics is committed to the God of the world and the word, the lordship of Christ, in dependence on the Holy Spirit, that seeks to advance intellectual support for the faith with reverence, gentleness, and humility for the edification of the saints.

## Reformed Classical Apologetics' Key Concepts

1. **Worldview** — It is the ultimate framework that organizes and justifies all of a person's consequential intellectual and existential commitments. It consists of an "interrelated cluster of central assumptions or presuppositions about reality" (Groothuis 2011: 74). Ronald Nash contends that the study of worldviews is vital because if someone views the world "through the wrong world-view, the world won't make much sense to him" (Nash 1988: 25).

2. **General revelation** — A class or type of God's self-revelation that includes the natural world, in logic, and in the human conscience, given generally or universally to all humanity. This revelation does not determine Christian doctrine and practice, but it does confirm the existence of special revelation. This forms the primary source material for arguments for the existence of God. (Groothuis 2023: 261). General revelation is called "general" for two reasons: (1) it is general in content, and (2) it is revealed to a general audience. It provides us with the knowledge that God exists. (Sproul, *Essential Truths of the Christian Faith*)

3. **Special revelation** — It is a class or type of God's self-revelation that includes the Bible, the person and work of Jesus Christ, and the Holy Spirit. It is the primary and definitive material from which we understand the truth about God, humanity, the good life, the world, and any other topic it addresses. It confirms natural theology. (Groothuis 2023: 261)

4. **Natural Theology** — It consists of theistic arguments (sometimes called theistic proofs). They are rational arguments for the existence of a monotheistic God that do not appeal to sacred scriptures for their cogency (Groothuis 2011: 171). We can say that this is the hallmark of Reformed Classical Apologetics.

5. **Common ground** — Any beliefs that Christians hold with those who are outside of the Christian faith that can serve as a way to help those outside the faith to embrace the gospel. For example, belief in one God, belief in objective morality, or belief in logic are kinds of common ground that Christians can discover and build on to help others to embrace the gospel. (Groothuis 2023: 262)

6. **Point of departure** — Contrary to common ground, the apologist must identify and drill down into the reasons for the disagreement and differences with the unbeliever. This may include a different understanding of causality, the idea of deity, and naturalism. It is the "exact ground that needs to be challenged."

7. **Proof** — *Christianity is reasonable.* It is true! This is the apologist's positive demonstration of the Christian faith or "the positive task of constructing a case for Christianity" (Sproul 2003). Reformed Classical apologetics has two components: rational/philosophical and evidential/contingent.

8. **Defense** (Negative Apologetics) — *Christianity is not unreasonable.* It is the vindication of the Christian faith against counterfeit claims. This focuses on addressing the unbelievers' false ideas or challenges to the Christian faith that they present during the apologetic encounter. We must deconstruct the false ideas or allegations of contradiction against the Christian faith. "The task of negative apologetics is to show that none of the challenges to Christian faith is fatal; none of the problems Christian theism appears to have provides really sufficient reasons why people should reject it." (Nash 1988: 26)

9. **Offense** (Positive Apologetics) — *Christianity refutes unreasonable thought.* Non-Christian thought is unreasonable and false. This refers to the refutation of the unbeliever's position and his inability to justify what he claims. It claims that Christianity alone is reasonable for people to hold and that it is wholly irrational to hold any other position than that of Christianity. "The role of positive apologetics is first to offer support for essential Christian beliefs. But because any proper assessment of the Christian faith requires that it be seen and appraised as a whole, the more important job of positive apologetics is to explain the details of the Christian system and offer reasons why that world-view is superior rationally, morally, and existentially to any alternative system." (Nash 1988: 26)

## Application

- We must be committed to honoring Christ in our dealings with unbelievers and the defense of the faith.
- We must be willing to see God's truth revealed both in nature and in Scripture as God-given tools to vindicate Christian theism.
- We must be willing to engage in laborious intellectual study to be ready to justify and defend our faith and refute unbelief.
- We must be mindful of the good of the church of Christ in our study of apologetics. Apologetics must be done in the context of the local church.

*To the Triune God Alone be the glory!*

## Notes

[^1]: Hence, we can argue that Presuppositionalism is not "the" only Reformed approach in Apologetics.
[^2]: Classical Apologetics is also used by non-Reformed theologians and philosophers (e.g., William Lane Craig and J.P. Moreland). Nonetheless, we can still benefit from their books and rigorous defense of the faith.
[^3]: This does not mean that we argue at a worldview level, as Presuppositionalists do. While the study of worldviews is important for contextualizing beliefs, classical apologetics maintains that intellectual engagement is most effective when founded on shared reason and specific evidence, rather than forcing a confrontation between two entirely different philosophical systems (worldviews). Despite the reality of the noetic effects of sin, we do not find an absolute antithesis between believers and unbelievers but a common ground.
