> These objections tend to be more concerned with how natural theology would distort the view we have of God or His revelation even if (or when) one comes to faith and begins to start doing the rest of their theology. It is also the case that some objections are not against natural theology in total but against a particular kind of natural theology. Since I defend classical natural theology, only such objections as are raised against it will be covered.
>
> — Matt Marino[^1]

I've been researching and reading materials that answer the common objections to classical apologetics. Matt Marino of Reformed Classicalist did a great service to the Classic Reformed Apologetics with his series. This apologetic approach is built upon the doctrine of Classical Theism.

Here are the seven objections as listed on the Reformed Classicalist's website, with the basic forms:

**[‟Inconclusive" or "Insufficient" Objections](https://www.reformedclassicalist.com/home/does-natural-theology-yield-insufficient-conclusions?rq=Natural%20Theological%20Conclusions)**

- Form 1: Natural theological arguments are inconclusive, and so are insufficient as a ground to infallible truth or the certainty promised in Scripture.
- Form 2: Natural theological conclusions are insufficient as to their specific content, demanding only a general, or non-Christian, theism.

**["Warranted Belief" Objections](https://www.reformedclassicalist.com/home/warranted-belief-objections)**

- Form 1: If one must justify (by logic or evidence) all beliefs foundational to those which are articles of faith, then the faith of most believers is unwarranted.
- Form 2: If premises must generally be more known than conclusions, and if most believers lack the time or ability to attend to such arguments, then natural theology is both logically and spiritually unfitting as a ground to Christian faith and understanding.

**["Intuitive over Discursive" Objections](https://www.reformedclassicalist.com/home/intuitive-over-discursive-objections)**

- Form 1: Additional discursive reasoning or arguments are either unnecessary, illegitimate, or both.
- Form 2: Additional discursive reasoning or arguments are superfluous.

**["Greater Than Scripture" Objections](https://www.reformedclassicalist.com/home/greater-than-scripture-objections)**

- Form 1: Clarification equals improvement concern. To ground systematic theology in knowledge outside of Scripture suggests a more "accurate summary" than, or "improvement" upon, Scripture. Objective natural theology is such a ground.
- Form 2: Conceptual conditions concern. To ground divine truth in objective natural knowledge outside of Scripture assumes an equality of sources of revelation, thus making the present consensus of natural reason (philosophy, science, morality) the ruler over the Scriptures.
- Form 3: Up from Scripture to "real studies" concern. To ground divine truth in a natural knowledge fit for philosophical inquiry leads to the theologian "graduating from" Scripture.

**["God of the Philosophers" Objections](https://www.reformedclassicalist.com/home/god-of-the-philosophers-objections)**

- Form 1: There are as many "natural theologies" (as well as "natural laws") as there are people, so for these to be foundational is to replace the one authoritative word of God with the innumerable mere opinions of man.
- Form 2: There is an inverse relationship between a metaphysical theology and a narrative and relational theology; or, God-in-Himself is incompatible with God-in-act, still more so with God-in-the-flesh.

**["Noetic Effects of Sin" Objections](https://www.reformedclassicalist.com/home/noetic-effects-of-sin-objection)**

- Form 1: As opposed to prelapsarian natural theology and redeemed natural theology, postlapsarian natural theology is the construct of "natural man," that is, man, not only blinded by sin but in rebellion against God.
- Form 2: If propositional knowledge is not equivalent to true belief (even if it entails true belief), and if such belief is unwarranted, then true natural theological propositions uttered by unbelievers need not constitute true belief.

**["Humbled Apologetics" Objections](https://www.reformedclassicalist.com/home/humble-apologetics-objections)**

- Form 1: Humility beyond proof. "Demonstrative natural theology is rooted in modernist reaction and unjustifiable epistemological claims."
- Form 2: Humility beyond power. "Natural theology that begins in 'the nature of things' is a baptism of present power structures in the way that God has made the world."
- Form 3: Humility via historical involvement. "Demonstrative natural theology was speculative, static, and far removed from both history and humanity. A workable natural theology should follow the narrative flow of the Bible, the old creation being filled with signposts to the new creation."

Whether Natural theology, as a preamble for faith, may or may not serve as a prolegomenon for dogmatics, it will be the subject of Matt's [upcoming doctoral dissertation](https://www.reformedclassicalist.com/home/8ty9aqsruievf0yq1h48ooh37msyoa?).
